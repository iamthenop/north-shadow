# Castles Do Not Float
### Part IV: The Curtain

For a time, the castle feels effortless.

The lights stay on. The gates open when asked. The halls remain warm. The machinery remains somewhere else.

## Distance

Platforms create distance between systems and the infrastructure that supports them.

Machines become services. Operations become automation. Responsibility becomes contracts. The system still runs, but fewer people see how.

That distance changes what is visible.

## Dependence

Over time, the platform stops feeling like support.

Capabilities come from it.  
Operations follow its rules.  
Architecture begins to assume it will always be there.

The castle is no longer just where the system runs.

It becomes part of the system.

## The Question

This is when a quiet question appears.

If the castle stands in the clouds, what holds it there?

Not metaphorically.

Architecturally.

Every system still has machinery. Every platform still runs on infrastructure. Every capability still depends on something beneath it.

Gravity did not disappear.

It simply moved somewhere else.

We just do not see what is holding everything up.

## Structural Reality

Systems do not escape the constraints of the world.

Infrastructure still exists. Machinery still runs somewhere. People still operate the systems that keep the castle standing.

Abstraction can move these things.

It cannot remove them.

The lord of the castle is still the lord of the castle whether it stands on the ground or appears to float in the sky.

Responsibility does not disappear with altitude.

Because castles do not float.
