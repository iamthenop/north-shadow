# Part 1: The Illusion of the Big Switch
### PQC Readiness the Boring Way: Sustainable Migration Without Heroics

When new cryptographic standards are published, the instinct is to imagine a decisive transition. A coordinated migration. A moment when old primitives are retired and new ones take their place.

Enterprise systems do not move that way.

There was no universal cutover from SHA 1. No single event that replaced RSA 1024 everywhere. No day when every endpoint began speaking a new protocol version. Each transition unfolded gradually. Some systems moved early. Others lagged. A few were isolated rather than replaced.

Cryptography sits deep in the machinery of an organization. In certificate authorities. In firmware. In hardware security modules. In long lived trust anchors. In vendor products that move on their own timelines.

It does not flip.

## No Countdown

There is no date when RSA turns into a pumpkin.
There is no visible clock counting down to sudden failure.
What exists instead is gradual wear.

- Research progresses.
- Standards mature.
- Hardware capabilities evolve.
- Adversaries accumulate capability over time.

Risk does not arrive as a single event. It accumulates.

In machinery, components rarely fail without warning. They degrade. Responsible operators replace aging parts during scheduled service, not after seizure.

Cryptography is no different.

NIST has formalized post quantum algorithms. The direction of travel has changed. The ecosystem will adjust over time.

The question is not whether change is coming.

The question is whether change is absorbed through routine lifecycle events.

- Certificates expire.
- Keys rotate.
- Hardware is refreshed.
- Applications are rebuilt.
- Vendors release new firmware.

Those are your service intervals.

There is no midnight transformation.
There is gradual wear.

Responsible operators plan accordingly.
