# Part V: Boring Transitions
### PQC Readiness the Boring Way

By winter, the pumpkin has shriveled.

The seeds are already gone.

The squirrel is asleep somewhere out of sight.

Nothing about the road suggests a dramatic event.

That is the point.

Successful infrastructure transitions rarely look memorable while they are happening.

There is no single moment when everything changes.

There is no clean scene of migration.

There is only steady replacement, absorbed through the same intervals that already govern certificates, keys, hardware, software, and trust.

Organizations that treat this as a crisis will struggle to schedule the work.

Organizations that treat it as maintenance will absorb the change through ordinary operations.

Over time, the system simply becomes different.

The most successful transitions rarely produce a memorable migration event.

They leave behind a system that still looks ordinary.
