# Part 4: What This Looks Like in a Real Organization
### PQC Readiness the Boring Way: Sustainable Migration Without Heroics

Organizations that are ready for post quantum cryptography rarely look dramatic from the outside.

There is no large migration program.
No dedicated war room.
No countdown dashboards.

Instead, cryptography evolves alongside normal operations.

## Rotation is Ordinary

Certificates expire regularly.
Keys are rotated without incident.
Automation exists where it should.
Manual procedures exist where it must.

Engineers are accustomed to renewal events. Systems are built with the expectation that credentials will change.

Because rotation is routine, cryptographic transitions do not feel exceptional.

## Algorithms Are Not Hard Wired

Applications rely on cryptographic libraries rather than embedding assumptions about algorithms, key sizes, or signature formats.

When primitives change, systems adapt through configuration and library upgrades rather than architectural redesign.

This does not eliminate work. It ensures that the work is contained.

## Lifecycle Events Carry Direction

When anchoring events occur, they are used intentionally.
A certificate authority rollover includes compatibility testing.
A hardware refresh includes evaluation of cryptographic capabilities.
Vendor conversations include roadmap discussions.
No event is treated as a crisis. Each one simply nudges the system forward.

## Progress is Subtle

From year to year, nothing dramatic happens.
But the fleet changes.
Legacy assumptions appear less often.
Rotation intervals shorten.
Systems tolerate larger keys and signatures.
Progress accumulates through small adjustments.

## The Outcome

There is no moment when the organization declares itself migrated.
Instead, cryptography evolves through normal service intervals.
Change enters through routine operations and spreads gradually.
Over time, the system simply becomes different.
