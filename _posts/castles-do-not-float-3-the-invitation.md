# Castles Do Not Float
### Part III: The Offer

As infrastructure became easier to consume, a new ecosystem emerged around it.

Platforms began offering entire systems as services. Databases could be provisioned instantly. Identity systems could be integrated with a few lines of configuration. Payments, messaging, analytics, and storage could all be accessed through APIs.

Capabilities that once required entire teams could now be adopted in days.

## Platforms

These platforms solved real problems.

Operating complex systems is expensive. Maintaining infrastructure requires expertise. Running global services demands constant attention.

Platforms offered a different model. They would operate the machinery so others could focus on building applications.

## The Offer

The offer was simple.

Take my hand.  
Come with us.

Your systems will be as new.

You will not need to sweep the floors. You will not need to tend the machines.

The castle is already built. Everything you need is here.

From the towers, the ground feels very far away.

## A Different Kind of Architecture

For many organizations, the arrangement made sense.

Why maintain infrastructure that others could operate more efficiently? Why invest in expertise that specialized platforms already possessed?

The systems still ran. The services remained available. In many cases, they became easier to reach and easier to expand. Builders could stay closer to the application and further from the machinery beneath it.

The platforms became foundations on which new systems could be built. Entire industries began to assemble themselves on top of them.

It altered where time was spent, where expertise accumulated, and where operational knowledge was allowed to thin out.

That was not a small change.

## The Castle in the Clouds

From the perspective of those building on these platforms, the change was remarkable.

Complex systems could be assembled quickly. Capabilities could be added without operating the machinery behind them.

The castle had not only risen into the clouds.

It was now furnished, maintained, and ready for anyone who wished to live there.
