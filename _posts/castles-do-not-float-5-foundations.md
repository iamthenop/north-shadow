# Castles Do Not Float
### Part V: Foundations

Once the curtain is pulled back, the castle looks different.

The halls are still warm.  
The lights still shine.  
The gates still open when asked.

But the question remains.

What holds the castle up?

---
## Staying Grounded

Some choose to keep their castles firmly on the ground.

They operate the machinery themselves.  
They maintain the walls and gates.  
They accept the weight of the system.

This approach is expensive.

But the structure is clear.

---
## Pillars

Others allow the castle to rise into the clouds.

But they anchor it to the earth with pillars.

Critical authority remains grounded.  
Certain responsibilities never leave the domain of the castle's lord.

In practice this often means the most sensitive operations do not occur inside the castle itself.

Key material may reside in hardware security modules (HSMs) that the castle cannot command.  
Critical operations may require systems that remain under the direct authority of the lord.

The castle may rise into the clouds.

But its authority is still planted in the ground.

---
## Cables

Some choose a different structure.

The castle is suspended by cables.

Multiple supports hold it in place.  
No single structure carries the entire weight.

In practice this often means authority is shared.

Some responsibilities may be handled by external platforms.  
Others remain under the direct authority of the castle's lord.

Critical actions require participation from more than one party.

The lord still rules the castle.

But the lord has chosen to govern with parliament.

The castle may remain in the clouds.

But its stability comes from the tension between the cables that hold it aloft.

---
## The Dream

Others remain content with the dream.

The castle appears to float.

The machinery stays hidden.  
The structure remains distant.

As long as the lights remain on and the gates continue to open, the question of foundations may never arise.

---
## Structural Reality

Castles do not float.

Some remain on the ground.  
Some are anchored by pillars.  
Some are suspended by cables.

But all of them are held up by something real.

The lord of the castle may choose how to live with gravity.

Gravity itself is not optional.
